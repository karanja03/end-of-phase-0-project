DID YOU KNOW??

  • Hair is made up of a protein called keratin, which is produced in hair follicles located in the scalp.
  • On average, hair grows about half an inch (1.25 cm) per month, or about 6 inches (15 cm) per year.
  • Factors like genetics, age, and overall health can affect hair growth and quality.
  • It's normal to lose around 50-100 hairs per day. Excessive hair
    loss may be a sign of an underlying issue and should be checked by a healthcare professional.
  • Maintaining a balanced diet rich in vitamins and minerals,
    particularly iron, zinc, and biotin, can promote healthy hair growth.
  • Overwashing your hair can strip away its natural oils and
    cause dryness. Aim to wash your hair 2-3 times per week or as needed.
  • Using lukewarm water to wash your hair is recommended as hot water can strip away natural oils.
  • Regularly conditioning your hair can help keep it moisturized
    and prevent breakage. Choose a conditioner that suits your hair type.
  • Avoid brushing wet hair vigorously as it is more prone to breakage.
    Instead, use a wide-toothed comb or a brush specifically designed for wet hair.
  • Heat styling tools like straighteners, curling irons,
    and blow dryers can damage your hair if used frequently. Limit their use and apply a heat protectant before styling.
